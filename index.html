Tunisia Data Literacy Program

As part of the World Bank’s ongoing effort to provide the Tunisian Government high quality support for producing and analyzing data and statistics, the Program aims to sustainably transfer data literacy skills to government officials, members of the media and academics, and to facilitate synergies in support of the decision-making process in Tunisia.

Designed under the TERI Umbrella Program, the courses are shaped to offer a modular online/onsite pedagogy both in English and French, and to share innovative open resources and tools with participants. Mentorship and bespoke trainings are also integrated into the Data Literacy Program, enabling concrete use of key data and statistics for specific economic and social research or national policy questions.

#TDLP

Expected Outcomes

GOVERNMENT OFFICIALS

Members of the government will familiarize with the use of official and alternative data sources and integrate them to improve the development of national legislation, governance, standards, skills and partnerships.

MEDIA

National media will share data-driven stories based on advanced data visualization to best report about the country. Training of trainers will be set up to create a culture of data.

ACADEMICS

Members of the universities and experts of the Tunisian statistical sector will empower their knowledge on data query and analysis and ensure sustainability towards embedding data literacy material into graduate and research higher education trainings.

COMMUNITY

Curriculum developed through this course will be published in French and English in an open-source format to be adapted and used for others to increase data literacy capacity.


Workshop for Government Officials

Overview of the Tunisia Data Literacy Program

Introduction on the main objectives of the Program and their importance for the Tunisian context and within the framework of the modernization of its national statistic system.

Art of the Possible - Official Statistics, New Data Sources and Administrative Data

An overview to better understand administrative data and their public function from the work of the UK Office for National Statistics.

Open Government Data and International Experiences to Reach Development Objectives

The value of using trustable open data for government policies improvement but also to boost private companies’ innovation towards new market opportunities.

Official Data and Statistics Produced and Shared by INS

Towards the democratization of a “data culture”, the INS presents the communication tools introduced to facilitate the understanding and use of Tunisian government data.

Data Policy in the Age of AI to Facilitate Use and Reuse of Data for Decision Making

With AI, the possibilities of what we can do with data have expanded exponentially, but they need to be trained to avoid risks. A glimpse over private and public sector uses.

Applied Research Training and Mentoring Program

How to implement and interpret advanced econometric techniques for real-world applications.

Practical Applications for Data Analysis in STATA

How to use STATA to process datasets and run econometric models.
